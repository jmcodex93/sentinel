02Render Passes

Three tiers, because comp
asks three questions.

Sentinel doesn't just switch Redshift passes on — it groups them by what a compositor actually needs from a frame, and tunes each one to how Nuke or After Effects reads it back.

Why grouped

A render pass is only useful if comp can read it. Sentinel writes the data passes lossless so their numbers survive, adapts Depth and Motion Vectors to the target compositor, and — above all — bundles them into tiers that map to the three things you ask of a render: rebuild it, take it apart, or relight it.

Essentials · 11 passes

Can comp rebuild the frame?

The floor. The Beauty as reference, the lighting components that add back to it, and the three technical passes every shot needs — Depth, Motion Vectors, Cryptomatte. Enough to reconstruct and finish.

Production · 17+ passes

Can comp take it apart?

Adds the raw and filter passes, World Position, Normals and AO — plus the Z Fog matte. Relight, retexture, add atmosphere: full control of the image after the render, no re-render.

Light Groups

Can comp relight it?

Splits the Beauty per light so each source is gradeable on its own in comp. One button diagnoses the groups in the scene and toggles them onto Beauty.

03Frame & Formats

One camera. Five deliveries. Zero re-framing by hand.

The Sentinel Frame is a single per-camera tag — the one entry point for multi-format delivery. It draws live viewport guides as you compose, then generates a camera-scoped Take per aspect with a true WYSIWYG crop.

9:16 shows its asymmetric safe insets baked in — 8 / 15 / 5 / 10 — the IG Reels caption & icon zones, not just the raw aspect crop.

  • Live guides. Toggle guides, a dimmed mask with opacity, and platform safe-zones — computed live in the master view as you compose.
  • One-click delivery Takes. A camera-scoped Take per format, rename-safe on re-run (tracked by BaseLink), reverted with a single Cmd+Z. A “Takes out of date” HUD warns when a setting changes.
  • True WYSIWYG crop. Narrower formats crop by a focal zoom — works on standard and Redshift cameras, keeping DOF; wider formats crop by resolution.
  • Per-format nudge. Shift each crop via camera film-offset — preserves lens and parallax, never moves the camera.

05The Last Mile

The render finished.
Sentinel checks that it's actually all there.

Scene Collector packages a clean delivery; Post-Render Validation audits the frames on disk against what the scene said it would produce. The gap between “render started” and “render correct” is where nights get lost.

Scene Collector

  • Pre-flight QC before anything is copied — the gate can auto-fix, accept into baseline, or stop.
  • Clean delivery naming — robot_010_v022_FINAL.c4d becomes robot_010.c4d; the manifest keeps the traceability.
  • Sidecars travel too — notes, baseline and the effective ruleset are copied and renamed into the delivery.

Post-Render Validation

  • Sequence gaps and 0-byte / truncated frames, flagged against the expected range.
  • Size-outlier detection (MAD) and previous-session “stale” clusters that shouldn't be in the delivery.
  • AOV coverage per Take and format — reading the scene's real render data, not a guess.

06Rules & Gates

One studio, one ruleset.

A project drops a sentinel_rules.json in the scene folder — or up to three ancestors — and the whole team validates against the same FPS, start frame, approved presets, safe-area insets and per-check severity. Precedence is explicit: project rules › machine settings › embedded defaults.

Per-project rules

Discovered, cached, live

Nearest file wins; shadowed files are surfaced in the header. Per-key validation rejects one bad value by name and applies the rest. Edits take effect without a restart.

Baseline

Accepted violations

Sign off a known issue with author + reason. The score then counts only new violations — a mixed row reads 2 new (1 accepted). Identity survives renames via object GUID.

Quality gates

Actionable at save & collect

Failing checks raise a triage dialog — auto-fix the batchable ones in a single undo step, accept into baseline, or proceed anyway. Off by default; opt in per project.

07Toolbox

The tedious parts, automated.

Texture Repathing, cross-platform snapshots and a full suite of scene tools — the parts of mograph delivery nobody wants to do by hand.

Texture Repathing

A multi-renderer bulk find/replace and smart-fix for texture paths — built for supervision (validate a whole scene's assets) and for artists (one-button fixes, undo-safe).

  • Scans every storage mechanism — Redshift & Arnold node graphs, classic shader chains, Octane image shaders, RS Dome HDR, Alembic caches, tag shaders.
  • Per-path status: OK · absolute · missing · asset URI · empty, with a status filter.
  • Case-insensitive find/replace with a Match-case toggle; the whole batch reverts with a single Cmd+Z.

Async, not modal — C4D stays interactive, so undo actually reaches the document.
